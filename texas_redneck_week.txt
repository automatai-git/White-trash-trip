TRIP OVERVIEW

Base: Fort Worth Stockyards area. The perfect home base — walkable honky tonks, weekly rodeo, live country music, and BBQ all within stumbling distance. Day trips out for the big-ticket adrenaline activities.

Dates: Tuesday June 16 (arrive) through Sunday June 21 (depart). 5 full activity days.

Weather: June in North Texas averages 33–37°C (91–99°F). Hot and humid. Early mornings and evenings are your best outdoor windows. Hydration and sunscreen are non-negotiable.

Important note on NASCAR: The 2026 NASCAR Cup Series at Texas Motor Speedway ran May 1–3. No major NASCAR event during your dates. However, the NASCAR Racing Experience (drive an actual stock car around TMS at 160mph) IS available year-round. Dirt track racing at local speedways runs Saturday nights.

DAY 1 — TUESDAY JUNE 16: ARRIVAL & FIRST BEER

Afternoon: Arrive & Check In

Fly into DFW International Airport. Rent 2–3 large SUVs or a 15-passenger van (essential for the group). Head straight to your accommodation in the Fort Worth Stockyards area. Hotel options below.

Late Afternoon: Fort Worth Stockyards

Walk Exchange Avenue. Catch the 4:00 PM Fort Worth Herd cattle drive — real longhorn cattle driven down the street twice daily. Free to watch, pure Texas photo op. Browse the western wear shops; everyone buys a cowboy hat. You’ll need them all week.

Evening: Steaks & Billy Bob’s

Dinner: Cattlemen’s Steak House or Lonesome Dove Western Bistro (chef Tim Love’s spot in the Stockyards). Get the bone-in ribeye. You’re in Texas.

After dinner: Billy Bob’s Texas — the world’s largest honky tonk. 120,000 sq ft, 30+ bar stations, live music, and a real indoor bull riding arena. Wednesday nights have free line dance lessons at 7 PM if you want to warm up. General admission ~$10–15. Mechanical bull riding is also available.

DAY 2 — WEDNESDAY JUNE 17: HOGS FROM HEAVEN

This is the crown jewel of the trip. Helicopter hog hunting with full-auto machine guns from the air, followed by a ground-based machine gun range session. All-day experience.

HeliBacon — Bryan, Texas

Location: 6120 E State Hwy 21, Bryan, TX 77808 (approx. 2.5 hours southeast of Fort Worth)

Phone: 877-889-7009

Website: helibacon.com

What You Get

HeliBacon operates a Bell 212 HP twin-engine helicopter — the largest aircraft used by any heli-hog hunting company. The experience includes:

1-hour mandatory safety course (NRA-certified instructor)

2-hour helicopter flight over 300,000+ acres of private ranchland

Suppressed AR-15 provided (full-auto upgrade available — DO THIS)

No bag limit on hogs. 30+ kills per flight is common

No Texas hunting license required for feral hogs on private land

4-camera video recording package available (strongly recommended)

Machine Gun Range (Afternoon Add-On)

After the heli hunt, the afternoon machine gun range experience is the perfect chaser. HeliBacon’s arsenal includes:

M134 Minigun (3,000 rounds/min — the Predator gun)

M2 ‘Ma Deuce’ .50 caliber heavy machine gun

MG42 ‘Hitler’s Buzzsaw’ (1,200 rpm)

MP5, Tommy Gun, Glock 18 machine pistol, RPD, M60, and more

All ammo, safety equipment, and instruction included

DAY 3 — THURSDAY JUNE 18: HEAVY METAL DAY

Since your group has already done ARs and pistols, today is about the weapons you’ve never touched: belt-fed machine guns, miniguns, .50 cal sniper rifles, and grenade launchers. Then PBR bull riding at night.

Location: 1901 W State Hwy 114, Grapevine, TX 76051 (20 min from Fort Worth, right next to DFW Airport)

Website: texasgunexperience.com

NSSF 5-Star rated facility with 200+ firearms in their rental inventory. Climate-controlled indoor range with 25 and 50-yard lanes. They specifically market to FIFA World Cup 2026 international visitors.

Afternoon: Chill & Explore

Buc-ee’s (if you didn’t stop yesterday): The most Texas gas station imaginable. 120 fuel pumps, a building the size of a warehouse, beef jerky wall, Texas souvenirs, and absurdly clean bathrooms. Fort Worth location or stop in Temple/Waco.

Go-kart racing: K1 Speed Fort Worth (indoor, electric, competitive) or Alley Cats in Arlington.

Zero Gravity Thrill Amusement Park (Dallas): 100-ft freefall, bungee, sky coaster. Cheap thrills.

Evening: PBR Stockyards Showcase

PBR (Professional Bull Riders) Stockyards Showcase runs every Thursday at 7:30 PM at Cowtown Coliseum in the Fort Worth Stockyards. This is dedicated bull riding — the most violent 8 seconds in sports. Intimate 3,400-seat arena where you’re close enough to feel the dirt. Beers, betting on riders with the boys, screaming.

After PBR: Walk to the Stockyards bars. White Elephant Saloon (oldest bar in Fort Worth, live music nightly) and Booger Red’s (dive bar with character).



DAY 4 — FRIDAY JUNE 19: TEXAS BBQ PILGRIMAGE

Friday is a road trip day. You’re hitting the absolute best BBQ in Texas, then racing back for the Stockyards Championship Rodeo.

Morning: Goldee’s Barbecue (Fort Worth)

Location: 4645 Dick Price Rd, Fort Worth, TX 76140

Texas Monthly’s #1 BBQ in Texas (2022 list). Open Friday–Sunday, 11 AM–3 PM. Get in line by 9 AM for 11 AM opening. The brisket is considered the definitive standard in Texas. Expect 2–3 hour wait on Fridays but the line IS the experience — bring a cooler with beers.

Afternoon Option A: Pecan Lodge (Dallas)

Location: 2702 Main St, Dallas (Deep Ellum neighborhood)

Best BBQ in Dallas proper. Michelin Bib Gourmand. Massive beef ribs, outstanding brisket, legendary mac and cheese. 30 min from Fort Worth. If Goldee’s fills you up, save this for another meal.

Afternoon Option B: Lockhart BBQ Capital Road Trip

For the truly committed: Lockhart, TX (3 hours south of Fort Worth) is the officially designated “Barbecue Capital of Texas.” Hit 2–3 joints back-to-back:

Kreuz Market (since 1900): No sauce, no forks. Meat on butcher paper. Post oak smoke.

Smitty’s Market (since 1948): Walk through the smoke pit to order. Sausage and pork ribs.

Black’s Barbecue (since 1932): Oldest family-run BBQ in Texas. Generous portions, great sausage.

Barbs B Q (Saturdays only): Newest kid in Lockhart but on Texas Monthly’s Top 50.

Honest assessment: The Lockhart trip is 6+ hours of driving round-trip. With your packed schedule, the smarter play is Goldee’s + Pecan Lodge (both within 30 min of your base). Save Lockhart for a future Texas road trip. But if the group WANTS the road trip experience, Lockhart is legendary and won’t disappoint.

Evening: Stockyards Championship Rodeo

Cowtown Coliseum, 7:30 PM. The world’s only year-round professional rodeo. Held in the arena that hosted the world’s first indoor rodeo in 1918. Events include: bull riding, bareback bronc, saddle bronc, barrel racing, tie-down roping, team roping, and breakaway roping. Tickets from ~$25–40/person.

After the rodeo, you’re already in the Stockyards — hit Billy Bob’s for live music. Friday nights have live bull riding inside the honky tonk.

The biggest day. Bass Pro Shop, Stockyards rodeo, and Billy Ray Cyrus at Billy Bob’s. Peak redneck achievement.

DAY 6 — SUNDAY JUNE 21: RECOVERY & DEPARTURE

Morning: Whataburger Breakfast

You cannot leave Texas without a Whataburger Honey Butter Chicken Biscuit for breakfast. This is the Texas equivalent of a religious experience. Hit the drive-through on your way out.

Midday: Buc-ee’s Final Stop

If you haven’t already, make a final stop at Buc-ee’s for souvenirs, beef jerky for the flight home, and kolaches (Czech-Texas pastries filled with sausage and cheese).

Afternoon: Fly Home

Depart DFW. Arrive in Norway sunburned, full of brisket, and with helicopter hog hunting footage that will make every group chat jealous for the rest of 2026.
